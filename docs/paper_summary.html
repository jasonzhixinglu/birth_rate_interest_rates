Birth Rate Collapse and Its Effect on Interest Rates

Author

Jason Lu and Coen Teulings

Published

April 6, 2026

1 Abstract

We study the macroeconomic consequences of the post-1970 fertility collapse using a large overlapping-generations model with CES production calibrated to Germany. A two-parameter fertility shock — capturing the permanent transition from a high to a low Total Fertility Rate — drives cohort-size dynamics. Solving for the transitional interest rate path via an iterative algorithm, we find that real interest rates fall steeply from around 1985, trough near 2035 overshooting the new balanced growth path level, and gradually recover thereafter.
